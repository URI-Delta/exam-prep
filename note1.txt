Q1 (Short Answers – Any 8, 5 Marks Each)
Answer any 8 of the following (I've provided all for completeness; select as needed).
• a) Define cyber security.
Cyber security is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and damage, using technologies,processes, and policies to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.
• b) Define Cyber crime.
Cyber crime refers to illegal activities conducted via the internet or digital devices, such as hacking, phishing, or data theft, aimed at financial gain,
disruption, or harm, often violating laws like the IT Act 2000.
• c) What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers impersonate trustworthy entities (e.g.,via fake emails or websites) to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.
• d) What is Cyber terrorism?
Cyber terrorism involves using digital means to intimidate or coerce governments or societies, such as hacking critical infrastructure (e.g., power grids) to cause fear or disruption, often for political motives.
• e) What is Steganography?
Steganography is the technique of hiding secret data within ordinary files (e.g., images or audio) to conceal its existence, unlike encryption which makes data unreadable but visible.
• f) What is reconnaissance?
Reconnaissance is the initial phase of a cyber attack where attackers gather information about a target (e.g., via social engineering or scanning tools) to identify vulnerabilities without direct engagement.
• g) Define attack vector.
An attack vector is the path or method used by attackers to exploit vulnerabilities, such as email attachments, USB drives, or unpatched software, to gain unauthorized access.
• h) What is denial-of-service (DoS) attack?
A DoS attack overwhelms a system or network with excessive traffic (e.g., using bots) to make it unavailable to legitimate users, disrupting services like websites.
• i) What is virus?
A virus is malicious software that replicates itself and spreads to other files or systems, often causing damage like data corruption or system crashes (e.g., the ILOVEYOU virus).
• j) What is online fraud?
Online fraud involves deceptive schemes on the internet, such as fake auctions or identity theft, to obtain money or personal data illegally, often via phishing or fake websites.
